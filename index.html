Welcome

Our group investigates how photosynthesis works, how it evolved, and how it can be redesigned. Using synthetic biology, genome engineering, physiology, and evolutionary approaches, we aim to understand and improve photosynthetic performance in plants.

We combine chloroplast biology, gene expression analysis, metabolic engineering, and regulatory genomics to explore the natural diversity of photosynthetic systems and to create new-to-nature variants.

Research Areas

Evolution of Photosynthesis

We study how C3, C4, and CAM photosynthesis originated and diversified, using comparative genomics and physiological analysis from model and non-model species.

Synthetic Photosynthesis

We engineer photosynthetic pathways—including carbon concentration mechanisms—to test new metabolic configurations and expand natural photosynthetic capabilities.

Chloroplast Gene Regulation

We investigate how nuclear and plastid genomes coordinate gene expression and how this affects photosynthetic function, stress responses, and development.

Genetic and Genomic Tools

We develop tools for plastid transformation, genome editing, promoter engineering, and reporter systems for photosynthetic gene networks.
