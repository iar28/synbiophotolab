Re-engineering the Future of Photosynthesis

Our group investigates how global re-arrangements of gene regulatory networks have shaped the evolution of carbon concentrating mechanisms like C4 photosynthesis and Crassulacean acid metabolism (CAM). We apply this knowledge to engineer novel photosynthetic pathways in plants, enhancing crop production under heatwaves, drought, and changing environmental conditions.

Leveraging genomics, phenomics, and synthetic biology, we bridge evolutionary insights with cutting-edge engineering to create resilient photosynthetic systems. C4 species excel in CO2 fixation but falter in extreme drought, while CAM plants thrive in aridity at the cost of productivity—we're redesigning the best of both worlds.

Research Lines

Evolution of Carbon Concentrating Mechanisms

Exploring the origins and diversification of C4 and CAM photosynthesis through comparative genomics, phenomics, and regulatory network analysis in model and wild species.

Synthetic Photosynthetic Pathways

Engineering hybrid metabolic configurations, including novel CO2 concentration modules, to boost photosynthetic efficiency and stress tolerance in crops.

Gene Regulatory Networks

Decoding nuclear-plastid coordination in gene expression, focusing on how regulatory rewiring drives photosynthetic adaptation and innovation.

Genomic Engineering Tools

Developing synthetic biology toolkits for plastid editing, promoter design, and high-throughput screening to accelerate photosynthetic redesign.
