Nature as Architect, Biology as Canvas

Like Gaudí found structural genius in trees, catenary curves, and light through leaves, we study how evolution sculpted photosynthetic masterpieces — C4 and CAM pathways — and use synthetic biology to re-imagine them for future crops.

Our laboratory merges evolutionary insight with genomic engineering to create plants that photosynthesise better under heat, drought, and rising CO₂ — turning the logic of nature into deliberate design.

Research Lines

Evolution of Carbon-Concentrating Mechanisms

Decoding how C4 and CAM photosynthesis emerged through regulatory rewiring — nature’s own Gaudí-esque solutions to aridity and light.

Synthetic Photosynthetic Pathways

Engineering hybrid CO₂-concentrating modules and diurnal circuits to give crops unprecedented resilience and yield.

Gene Regulatory Networks

Uncovering the genomic “catenary arches” that coordinate nucleus and chloroplast during development and stress.

Genomic & Synthetic Tools

Developing plastid transformation systems, mosaic promoters, and high-throughput phenotyping platforms.
